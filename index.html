ML Model

The Dataset for the Model

The dataframe is fairly simple. I have added my calories data and my stepcount data together with my months and days. Also with the helpof my months and days I identified the school days and marked the days I do not have school as 1.

Logistic Regression

In order make an decisive analysis of the data. I made an logistic regression for school days because the values were either 1 or 0. As we can see from the graph the places where step count is over 10000 is generally classified as non school days so it is proving my point. The values where both calories burned and step count high are not school days however days where my calories burned is high and stepcount is low are school days.

Correlations

In the correlations heatmap we see these results again. School is highly correlated with the amount of steps I take in a day. We see here again that I am more active during non school days. Also I wanted to put months and days here just to see how they effect my step count. They are both positively correlated especially month but not that highly correlated and result with calories burned is expected because the more you burn calories means that you walked more.

Confusion Matrix

I wanted to see which days are confused with which and it seems that non school days are confused with school days the most and school days are nearly never confused with non school days which is really interesting.

The Decision Tree

Now lets see how the decision tree interperets this data. First of all in the first split We can see that it classifies step counts less than 12k as school days and rest as non school days. In the right it classifies calories less than 295 are all school days so the branching stops and it classfies the rest as non school days. The main pattern that follows in this tree is that when we are working with high calories burned and high step count it always classifies it as non school days and the rest as school days. So, we can conclude it directly proves my point.
